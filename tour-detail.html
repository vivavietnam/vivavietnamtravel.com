5 Trade Streets of Saigon — A Night with the Locals

★★★★★ 4.9 (128 reviews)
3 hours
Max 8 guests
District 1, Ho Chi Minh City
Daily departures
English & Vietnamese

Forget the tourist traps. The 5 Trade Streets of Saigon is your chance to eat the way the locals do — in alleyways lit by bare bulbs, at plastic-stool restaurants that haven't changed in 40 years, guided by someone who grew up eating here.

We start as the sun drops, when the city shifts gears and the street-food vendors wheel their carts into position. Over three hours, you'll taste your way through five of Saigon's most celebrated dishes — from the smoky intensity of bún bò Huế to the delicate sweetness of chè ba màu — at the spots our guides have been eating at since childhood.

This is a walking tour. The pace is relaxed and social. You'll meet the vendors, hear the stories behind each dish, and leave with a full stomach and a genuine feel for how Saigon actually tastes.

What makes this special
  • All 5 food stops chosen by guides who eat here weekly — not from a tourist list
  • Small group of maximum 8 means personal attention and no crowds
  • You'll try 5 distinct dishes including one "secret" item not on our published menu
  • Each vendor stop includes a short story about the dish's history in Vietnamese cuisine
  • Ends at a rooftop beer bar with panoramic District 1 views
6:00 PM
Meet at Bến Thành Market
Meet your guide at the south entrance of Bến Thành Market. A quick intro to the evening's route and what to expect. We set off just as the night market opens.
6:20 PM
Stop 1 — Bún Bò Huế Alley
A family-run stall operating since 1978. Their bún bò Huế is made from a 12-hour broth and served with a generous side of fresh herbs. This is the real thing.
🍜 Taste Stop
6:55 PM
Walk through Hẻm 76 — The Hidden Alley
A narrow laneway locals call "the food street that tourists haven't found." Your guide will point out the architectural mix of French colonial and modern Vietnamese design as we walk through.
7:20 PM
Stop 2 — Bánh Xèo at Mrs. Thủy's
Mrs. Thủy has been flipping her sizzling Vietnamese crêpes (bánh xèo) here for 22 years. You'll watch her work, then wrap yours in fresh lettuce and herbs the way it's meant to be eaten.
🥞 Taste Stop
7:50 PM
Stops 3 & 4 — Sweet Side of Saigon
A Vietnamese sandwich (bánh mì) from the cart that's been on this corner for 35 years, followed by a chè ba màu dessert — the three-colour sweet bean pudding that has no equal anywhere else.
🧋 Taste Stop
8:30 PM
Stop 5 — The Secret Spot 🤫
We don't reveal this one on purpose. Every guest has a different reaction. This is the moment most people say they'll remember longest.
9:00 PM
Rooftop Finale — Bia Hơi with a View
We end on a rooftop bar with views across District 1. First round of bia hơi (draft beer) is on us. This is where the group usually stays long after the tour officially ends.

What should I wear? +
Comfortable walking shoes are essential — you'll cover about 2.5km on mixed surfaces. Light, breathable clothing is best. Bring a light rain jacket during the wet season (May–November).
Is this tour suitable for vegetarians? +
Yes! All 5 stops have vegetarian alternatives. Please mention your dietary requirements when booking and we'll let your guide know in advance so everything is arranged.
